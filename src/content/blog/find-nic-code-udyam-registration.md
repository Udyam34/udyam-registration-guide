When you register on the Udyam portal, you're asked to pick a **NIC code** for your business activity — and it's one of the most common places applicants get stuck. Choosing the wrong one can misclassify your enterprise. Here's what the NIC code is, how to find the right one, and what to do if you've already chosen incorrectly.

## What is a NIC code?

NIC stands for **National Industrial Classification** — the Government of India's system for categorising business activities. During Udyam registration you select the NIC code(s) that match what your business actually does (manufacturing, trading, services, etc.). The code determines how your enterprise is classified and can affect which schemes you're eligible for.

## Why picking the right code matters

- It records your **primary business activity** on your Udyam certificate.
- An inaccurate code can create mismatches if your registration is cross-checked against GST or other records.
- Some MSME benefits and tenders reference specific activity categories.

You can add **multiple NIC codes** if your business spans more than one activity (say, both manufacturing and trading).

## How to find the correct NIC code

1. Write your main activity in plain words (e.g., "stitching and selling readymade garments").
2. On the Udyam portal, use the NIC code search and type a keyword from that description.
3. Pick the most specific code that matches — NIC codes run from broad 2-digit groups down to detailed 5-digit activities.

If you're unsure which level fits, it helps to [find the correct NIC code](https://www.udyam-registration.com/find-nic-code/) with a lookup tool before you start, so you're not guessing inside the application.

## Chose the wrong code? You can fix it

A wrong NIC code isn't permanent. You can [update your Udyam registration](https://www.udyam-registration.com/edit-udyam/) to correct the code or add activities as your business grows — your Udyam number stays the same.

## FAQ

**Can I add more than one NIC code?** Yes — add every activity your business genuinely performs; there's no fixed limit.

**Does the NIC code affect my Micro/Small/Medium status?** No — that's based on investment and turnover, not the NIC code. The code records *what* you do, not your size.

**Where do I see my NIC code later?** It's listed on your Udyam certificate alongside your registration details.
